Who am I, in 24 seconds

A Product & Growth Designer who designs for the moment someone decides to say yes.

Based in Toronto, I work across research, UX/UI, and growth to help B2B and consumer teams understand their users and design for adoption, from global SaaS platforms to scrappy freelance sprints. My work and personal life run on the same current: stay curious, stay close to the data, and keep iterating. Taste is something I own, grounded in training and research, not preference. I use it to make the judgment call, while AI helps me open up more doors along the way.

Guiding philosophy

Principles behind every project

01

Empathetic Design Thinking

At the heart of my design process is a deep commitment to empathy. By thoroughly understanding user perspectives, I craft solutions that genuinely connect — using strategic methods to advance projects and make data-informed design decisions.

  • —Gathering in-depth user insights through comprehensive research
  • —Developing user personas and journey maps
  • —Organizing team activities to build empathy and understanding
  • —Regularly assessing design choices against user requirements
02

Iterative Design Process

My design process is iterative. I constantly refine solutions using new insights and feedback — a method that effectively reduces risk and enhances the product over time.

  • —Quick prototype and test cycles
  • —Practical "try and improve" approach
  • —Using data to guide design choices
  • —Seeing every result as a chance to get better
03

Collaborative Innovation Practice

I thrive on collaborative innovation. My team spirit and curious mindset help me adapt to changing needs, encouraging diverse viewpoints and cross-departmental teamwork so ideas can grow.

  • —Understanding how different elements interact
  • —Weighing how decisions play out long-term
  • —Teaming up with people from different fields
  • —Matching user needs with business goals and technical limits
04

Holistic Systems Thinking

I tackle design challenges from a systems perspective, considering the broader context and interconnections. This holistic view helps create more sustainable and impactful solutions.

  • —Mapping how touchpoints, teams and systems connect
  • —Weighing the long-run impact of design decisions
  • —Partnering across disciplines to see the full picture
  • —Balancing sustainability with immediate business needs
